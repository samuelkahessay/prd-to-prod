A two-beat

You describe it. The pipeline ships it.

From a meeting, a transcript, or raw notes — to deployed code. No human writes implementation. The pipeline heals itself when things break.

Canary pattern. Two beats. "You" establishes the human boundary. "The pipeline" establishes the autonomy.

B failure-mode

Your pipeline broke at 2am. It fixed itself by 2:12.

Autonomous software delivery that writes, reviews, deploys, and self-heals — from a meeting or a raw brief. No human writes code.

Moda pattern. Names the failure everyone fears, then resolves it with a specific number. The "12 minutes" is real from your drill data.

C category-defining

Autonomous software delivery. From brief to production.

Write what you want. The pipeline extracts requirements, creates issues, dispatches agents, reviews code, deploys — and fixes itself when things break.

Corelayer pattern. Defines the category in four words, then scopes it. "Brief to production" covers both meetings and raw text without naming either.

D wave-positioning

AI writes your code. This pipeline runs the rest.

Extraction, issue creation, review, deploy, and self-healing — the full lifecycle, autonomous. You write the brief. The system does everything else.

Acknowledges the AI coding wave (Copilot, Claude Code) but positions prd-to-prod as the orchestration layer on top. "The rest" = everything nobody else does.
