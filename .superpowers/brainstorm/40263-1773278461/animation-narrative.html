The pipeline story, in 5 acts

Each act plays sequentially in a ~18 second loop. The density builds as work fans out, then converges at review/deploy. The self-heal loop is the dramatic climax.

Act 1
Chaos → Clarity
Scattered particles (noisy meeting data) drift in from the left. They converge and compress into a single clean node: the PRD.
0s – 3s
Act 2
Fan-out
PRD pulses, then splits into 4-5 issue particles that spread vertically. Each finds its own lane. Dependency lines connect them (some wait for others).
3s – 6s
Act 3
Execution Wave
Issues get picked up in dependency order (not all at once). Each spawns a small burst (agent working). PRs appear as new particles heading toward Review. This is the dense phase — multiple agents working simultaneously.
6s – 11s
Act 4
Converge → Deploy
PR particles converge at a Review gate (diamond). Brief pause (human approval moment). Then all flow together into a single Deploy node that pulses green.
11s – 14s
Act 5
Break → Heal
Deploy node flickers red. A failure particle drops down into the heal loop. It files a bug, spawns a fix agent, the fix travels back up through review and re-deploys. Green pulse. Loop restarts.
14s – 18s

Density curve

Act 1 starts sparse (5-8 scattered particles). Act 3 is the peak (15-20 active particles across lanes). Act 4 converges back to few. The density itself tells the story — "a lot is happening in the middle."

Color mapping

Meeting chaos = muted grey. PRD = blue (accent). Issues = blue dots with lane trails. Agents = small grey bursts. PRs = blue moving right. Review gate = pulsing diamond. Deploy = green. Heal loop = warm red/orange.

The "wow" moment

Act 5. The deploy node going red, the failure particle dropping down, the fix racing back up — this is the moment that makes someone say "holy shit, it fixes itself." It should feel fast and decisive, not slow.

Idle state

After the loop plays 2-3 times, it can settle into a gentle ambient mode with light particles flowing along the established paths. The narrative grabs attention; the ambient keeps it from being distracting.
